--- POST 6 | LINKEDIN | Story ---

A gym owner named Tanya came to us last March with a problem that sounded simple: "I can't keep members."

Her gym was generating 80+ new leads a month. Marketing was strong. But her retention rate was devastating — 60% of new members cancelled within 90 days.

When we dug in, the real issue wasn't the gym experience. Members loved the classes. The problem was everything surrounding the experience:

- New members got zero automated onboarding. No welcome sequence. No check-in at day 7, 14, or 30.
- Missed class? Nobody followed up. The member just... disappeared.
- Billing issues went unresolved for days because they sat in an inbox nobody owned.
- Review requests? Never sent. Tanya had 29 Google reviews after 4 years.

We didn't change her classes or her pricing. We built systems around what was already great:

- Automated onboarding sequence: welcome email, day-3 check-in, day-7 "how's it going" text, day-14 personal invite to a signature class
- Missed-class trigger: if a member skips 5+ days, an AI-powered re-engagement sequence launches automatically
- Billing resolution bot: handles 80% of billing questions without staff involvement
- Post-class review request: automated text after every 10th visit

Six months later:
- 90-day retention went from 40% to 78%
- Google reviews: 29 to 214
- Monthly revenue increased by $22,000 — not from new members, but from keeping the ones she already had
- Tanya cut her ad spend by 30% because she stopped needing to replace churned members

Retention isn't a people problem. It's a systems problem. And when you build the right systems, growth takes care of itself.
